Custom administration of the Netherlands

Supervise the cross-border movement of goods

Role
UX/UI Design
Company
The Ministry of Finance
Year
2022

The primary task of the Customs Administration of the Netherlands is to supervise the cross-border movement of goods. Their main strategic objectives are Taks levy and collection, protection and boosting the competitive position of the Netherlands and Europe.

The Excise Movement and Control System (EMCS) is a computerised system for monitoring the movement of excise goods under duty suspension in the EU. It records, in real-time, the movement of alcohol, tobacco and energy products for which excise duties have still to be paid. More than 100 000 economic operators currently use the system, and it is a crucial tool for information exchange and cooperation between Member States.

With the system being technically outdated in the Netherlands, it was time to redesign the system and to review the interactions and functions. The EMCS system in the Netherlands consists of a service portal, but also the forms in which it is possible to register the movement of excise goods and actions.

With this system being outdated, it gave us the chance to align the portal with the new BOLD- design system. Automaticly making it accesible, consistent and clear. We also seized the moment, to create more synergy between the user requirements and that of the stakeholders. In order to achieve this we interviewed users and did design sessions with stakeholders. The insights were translated into the designs of the portal and the forms, afterwards we validated them. Utimatly, making the system more efficient, modern (responsive) and aligned.

Activities and deliverables

Gathering requirements, User research, Stakeholder management, Wireframes, Responsive design, Prototyping, Working with the design-system, UX/UI Design
